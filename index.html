Roman Matvienko

I am currently a PhD sociology student at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, writing a dissertation on association football. It is a video-based ethnomethodological study. Through a detailed analysis of match footage, I demonstrate that recognizing and anticipating the actions of others is crucial for the successful execution of both simple plays and complex team efforts. The study aims to provide an empirically grounded, interactionist perspective on coordination, antagonism, and decision-making.

I played amateur football and rugby union but have now retired to leisurely walking and non-competitive cycling. Outside of sport, my interests include science and crime fiction, urban nature, and tactical video games.
