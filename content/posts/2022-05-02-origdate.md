**tl;dr** Fill 'Extra' field in Zotero with `original-date: (year)` to
include original publication date in a reference.

Many publication manuals, APA and ASA included, require authors to
include the original publication date for republished and translated
sources. Zotero does not have a specific field for it, but it can be 
specified in the 'Extra' field, using the prefix `original-date:`.

For example, _Sport und Arbeit_ by Bero Rigauer, originally published
in German in 1969, was
[translated](https://archive.org/details/sportwork0000riga) to English
in 1974. 

After adding `original-date: 1969` to the 'Extra' field, Zotero
creates a correct reference, depending on a citation style:

- APA 7th edition
  - (in-text citation) Rigauer (1969/1981) 
  - (bibliography entry) Rigauer, B. (1981). _Sport and Work_ (A.
    Guttman, Trans.). Columbia University Press. (Original work
    published 1969)
- ASA 6th edition
  - (in-text citation) Rigauer ([1969] 1981)
  - (bibliography entry) Rigauer, Bero. [1969] 1981. _Sport and Work_.
    New York: Columbia University Press.

This tip works for Zotero extensions for LibreOffice and Microsoft 
Word, as well as for exporting citations to BibLaTeX using Better
BibTeX extension (it is important to export your library to 'Better
BibLaTeX' rather than 'Better BibTeX' format).

I have found this tip on Zotero [forum discussion](https://forums.zotero.org/discussion/63774/original-dates-of-publication).
