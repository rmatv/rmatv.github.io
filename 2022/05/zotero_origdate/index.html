Original publication date in Zotero

2 May 2022

tl;dr Fill ‘Extra’ field in Zotero with original-date: (year) to include original publication date in a reference.

Many publication manuals, APA and ASA included, require authors to include the original publication date for republished and translated sources. Zotero does not have a specific field for it, but it can be specified in the ‘Extra’ field, using the prefix original-date:.

For example, Sport und Arbeit by Bero Rigauer, originally published in German in 1969, was translated to English in 1974.

After adding original-date: 1969 to the ‘Extra’ field, Zotero creates a correct reference, depending on a citation style:

  • APA 7th edition
    • (in-text citation) Rigauer (1969/1981)
    • (bibliography entry) Rigauer, B. (1981). Sport and Work (A. Guttman, Trans.). Columbia University Press. (Original work published 1969)
  • ASA 6th edition
    • (in-text citation) Rigauer ([1969] 1981)
    • (bibliography entry) Rigauer, Bero. [1969] 1981. Sport and Work. New York: Columbia University Press.

This tip works for Zotero extensions for LibreOffice and Microsoft Word, as well as for exporting citations to BibLaTeX using Better BibTeX extension (it is important to export your library to ‘Better BibLaTeX’ rather than ‘Better BibTeX’ format).

I have found this tip on Zotero forum discussion.
